ğŸ¥ Case Study: Health Management Information System (HMIS)

Overview

The Health Management Information System (HMIS) is a strategic initiative designed to consolidate procurement and inventory data across healthcare facilities within the UPA network. It aims to replace fragmented legacy systems with a centralized, digital platform, enhancing efficiency in stock monitoring, reporting, and emergency preparedness across more than 120 health centers.

Goal

To establish a real-time, centralized health inventory and procurement system that integrates national-level data, providing transparent, automated reporting. This will lead to improved resource management, streamlined supply chain operations, and enhanced emergency response capabilities.

Objectives
  • Consolidate procurement and inventory data from 7 disparate legacy systems, including the Pharmacy Management System (PMS), Inventory Tracking System (ITS), and Procurement Database (PDB), into a unified HMIS database.
  • Eliminate manual reporting processes through automation, replacing paper-based reports with real-time dashboards and automated reporting tools, reducing reporting time by 75%.
  • Enable real-time visibility of stock levels (medicines, PPE, equipment), providing up-to-the-minute insights into inventory levels for essential medical supplies, personal protective equipment, and critical medical equipment across all health centers.
  • Alert health centers and the Ministry of Health about low-stock or emergency supply needs through automated SMS and email alerts, ensuring timely replenishment of critical supplies and enabling proactive response to emergencies.
  • Provide interactive dashboards for real-time monitoring and strategic planning, offering customizable views and data visualization tools to support informed decision-making at the facility, regional, and national levels.
  • Improve coordination between UPA and MoH for medical supply chain governance, facilitating seamless data exchange and collaborative decision-making to optimize resource allocation and supply chain efficiency.
Scope
  • Nationwide health facilities and MoH supply chain: Encompassing all UPA-affiliated health facilities and the MoH supply chain network, ensuring comprehensive coverage of the national healthcare system.
  • 120+ health centers included in live monitoring: Integrating data from over 120 health centers into the HMIS platform, providing real-time insights into inventory levels, procurement activities, and supply chain performance.
  • Integration with Ministry of Health systems: Establishing seamless data exchange between HMIS and existing MoH systems, such as the National Health Registry and Disease Surveillance System, to enhance data sharing and collaboration.
  • Dashboards for decision-makers at UPA and MoH: Providing tailored dashboards for decision-makers at UPA and MoH, offering customizable views, data visualization tools, and real-time insights to support strategic planning and resource allocation.
  • Automated alerts for inventory replenishment: Implementing automated alerts for inventory replenishment, notifying health centers and MoH operators when stock levels fall below pre-defined thresholds, ensuring timely replenishment of critical supplies.
Stakeholders
  • Ministry of Health: The primary government body responsible for healthcare policy and regulation, relying on HMIS for data-driven decision-making and resource allocation.
  • Unified Procurement Authority (UPA): The central procurement agency responsible for sourcing and distributing medical supplies, utilizing HMIS to optimize procurement processes and supply chain efficiency.
  • Health facility managers: The frontline healthcare providers responsible for managing inventory levels and ensuring adequate supply of essential medical supplies, relying on HMIS for real-time monitoring and automated alerts.
  • IT & data migration teams: The technical experts responsible for integrating legacy systems, migrating data, and ensuring the ongoing maintenance and support of the HMIS platform.
  • Emergency logistics planners: The personnel responsible for coordinating emergency response efforts and ensuring timely delivery of medical supplies to affected areas, relying on HMIS for real-time monitoring and situational awareness.
  • Data analytics and reporting teams: The data specialists responsible for analyzing HMIS data, generating reports, and providing insights to support decision-making at all levels of the healthcare system.
