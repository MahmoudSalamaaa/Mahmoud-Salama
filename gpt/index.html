Department of Systems, Applications & Technical Support

Used Methodologies

Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

The SDLC process comprises the following phases:

  • Phase 1: Requirement Analysis - Engage stakeholders, gather requirements, and deliver Business Requirements Document (BRD) and Software Requirements Specification (SRS).
  • Phase 2: System Design - Translate requirements into architecture, wireframes, and database schemas.
  • Phase 3: Development - Coding, API integration, and version control.
  • Phase 4: Testing - Unit, integration, system testing, and user acceptance testing.
  • Phase 5: Deployment - Production rollout and migration.
  • Phase 6: Maintenance - Continuous updates, bug fixes, and performance monitoring.

Impact on Organizational Goals

Our department acts as a critical enabler of UPA’s vision by embedding digital intelligence, operational agility, and technical excellence across all functions. Our initiatives directly accelerate UPA's ability to meet national objectives and deliver public value.

  • Driving Government Digitization: Consolidating fragmented legacy systems into unified interoperable platforms to support smart government and digital economy.
  • Enabling Strategic Decision-Making: Delivering dashboards and analytics for clear visibility and proactive planning.
  • Building Institutional Resilience: Robust cybersecurity, disaster recovery, and system availability to ensure continuity during crises.
